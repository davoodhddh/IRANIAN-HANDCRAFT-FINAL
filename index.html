History

Shiraz is a city with a rich history, dating back to the Achaemenid Empire over 2500 years ago. This long history has made Shiraz a center of Persian culture, literature, and art. Over the centuries, the city has attracted poets, scholars, and artists from around the region who have contributed to its vibrant artistic and cultural scene. One of the most important handcrafts produced in Shiraz is metal engraving, particularly on silver and copper. This art form has been practiced in Shiraz for centuries and has been passed down from generation to generation. Shirazi metal engraving is characterized by its intricate patterns and designs that are meticulously carved into the surface of the metal. The artisans who practice this craft use a variety of tools to create their designs, including hammers, chisels, and engraving needles. Alongside its cultural and artistic heritage, Shiraz is also known for its beautiful gardens, including the famous Eram Garden, which dates back to the 13th century. The garden features a central pool surrounded by fruit trees, flowers, and fragrant shrubs. The garden's design reflects the Persian tradition of creating outdoor spaces that blend beauty and function. Today, the garden is a popular tourist attraction and a symbol of Shiraz's rich cultural heritage.
